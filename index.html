I am a PhD candidate in Electrical Engineering at IIT Gandhinagar (GPA: 9.58/10), working under Prof. Nithin V. George. My research spans adaptive beamforming, direction-of-arrival estimation, and spatial audio processing for microphone array systems. I was a Visiting Research Fellow at Technion – Israel Institute of Technology, supported by the Viterbi Fellowship, collaborating with Prof. Israel Cohen on time-domain region-of-interest beamforming.

Research

DOA Estimation Coarray Adaptive Filtering

Adaptive Underdetermined DOA Estimation

Developed the Coarray LMS algorithm, capable of resolving more sources than physical sensors. Introduced a polynomial-rooting method for efficient spectral peak localization and an integrated covariance matrix error removal strategy for improved accuracy.

Beamforming ROI Spatial Audio

Region-of-Interest Beamforming

Developed a generalized sidelobe canceller framework for time-domain region-of-interest beamforming, enabling flexible spatial region selection for immersive audio capture. Work conducted in collaboration with Technion – Israel Institute of Technology.

Low-Rank Kronecker NLMS / RLS

Low-Rank Adaptive DOA Estimation

Proposed adaptive DOA estimation algorithms using complex Kronecker product decomposition, enabling parallel adaptive updates via NLMS and RLS for faster convergence and reduced computational complexity in large-scale sensor arrays.

Fourier Domain Complex LMS Sparsity

Fourier-Domain Adaptive DOA Estimation

Designed a family of Fourier-domain complex least square and fourth-order algorithms for efficient adaptive direction-of-arrival estimation, with sparsity-constrained variants using a variable penalty factor for improved resolution.

LEO Satellite Geolocation Planar Array

Ground-Based Source Localization via LEO Satellite

In collaboration with the Space Applications Centre, Ahmedabad — developed algorithms to estimate the geolocation of ground emitters using signals captured by planar arrays onboard low Earth orbit satellites.
